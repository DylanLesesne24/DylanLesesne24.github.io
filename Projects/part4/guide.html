PC Building Guide

Now that you’ve learned about the components, here’s how to choose compatible parts and put them together into a working PC build.

Choosing a CPU and Motherboard

Make sure your CPU matches the motherboard socket type. Intel and AMD use different sockets, so compatibility is crucial.

Selecting RAM

Motherboards support specific RAM types (DDR4, DDR5) and speeds. Check the maximum supported capacity before buying.

Pairing a GPU with a Power Supply

Graphics cards require specific amounts of power. Choose a PSU with enough wattage and the correct PCIe connectors.

Picking Storage

SSDs offer faster performance than HDDs. Many motherboards now support NVMe drives directly.

Case and Cooling

Ensure your case fits your motherboard (ATX, Micro-ATX, Mini-ITX). Also plan airflow and cooling for stable performance.
